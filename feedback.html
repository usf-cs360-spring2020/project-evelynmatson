Feedback

Live Feedback from Instructor

I met with my professor Sophie Engle over Zoom to get her feedback on the beta version of my prototype (the second half of our course was taught online due to the SARS-CoV-2 pandemic, so meeting in-person wasn't possible). She helped me decide which kinds of interactivity to implement, and suggested a few other tweaks for me to make. Here are her main ideas, summarized:

  • Adding details-on-demand interactivity in the form of a hover tooltip over countries would be helpful. This tooltip could include details about each of the country's 'explainor' factors that contribute to happiness.
  • The color scale used on the countries as displayed on the map, which encodes their calculated happiness values, could be used on the bars in the bar chart too. This would provide some continuity between the two charts, and help emphasize the connection between them.
  • Zooming would also be nice sometimes, but probably the least interesting kind of interactivity to implement.

Online Feedback from Peers

Additionally, I received written feedback on my beta prototype from three of my peers in my class, in the form of a rubric on Canvas with comments for each topic (like visual encodings, lie factor, gestalt principles, etc.). Many of their comments fit with what Sophie suggested, and helped inform what changes I made for the final version. Here are some of their most helpful comments:

    • "The planned interactivity would really help distinguish the countries and help for a better view."
    • "From these visualizations, I can see I should move to Finland and avoid Afghanistan but in reality, it really helped to see some ways people [categorize] a country's happiness and how it compares to other countries ...
