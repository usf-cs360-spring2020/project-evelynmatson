Noise Reports in San Francisco

Reported in February 2020, separated by category.

About

This is a non-proportional symbol map geographic visualization, displayed using a conic equal area projection. I have grouped the different types of reports into four broad categories, which then is encoded by color (see legend). I have also added outlines of the different neighborhoods in San Francisco, as defined by the Mayor's Office. This enables comparison between neighborhoods, and makes finding reports near specific parts of the city easier.

Each dot represents a single noise report, placed onto the map by its geographic location. Each dot is partially transparent, enabling multiple reports in the same location to appear more pronounced than singular ones.

  • Color of Dot - Broad category of the report. See legend.
  • Location of Dot on Map - Location of the report.

Interactivity

I have enabled both details-on-demand interactivity and filtering interactivity.

When hovering over different neighborhoods on the map, a details pane will appear which lists the number of incidents from different categories that happened in that neighborhood. The neighborhood will also be highlighted in red, making its boundaries clearer.

By clicking on the colored swatches in the legend, you can toggle each category of report to filter the data to only show the types you are interested in.

Conclusions

The visualization makes it clear that more noise reports happen near the downtown region of the city, and fewer happen in the west and south parts of the city. It also suggests that each type are about equally distributed throughout the city, which surprised me a little.
