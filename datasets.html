World Happiness Report 2020

The main dataset I will use will be from the 2020 World Happiness Report. This is an annual publication by the United Nations Sustainable Development Solutions Network that tracks people's happiness by country, and analyzes which factors contribute to or take away from people's happiness.

Accessed

April 12, 2020

Total Rows

2,195

Relevant Columns

Column Name Description
Country name Name of the country.
Example: Iceland
Regional indicator More specific type of incident.
Example: construction_private_property
Ladder score Happiness score of the country on the Cantril Ladder Scale.
Example: 7.50450
Logged GDP per capita Gross Domestic Product per capita of the country on a logarithmic scale.
Example: 10.77256

License

The World Happiness Report doesn't explicitly list the license their data is offered under, but they make their data easily accessible to the public as a CSV file, and give a citation for the report as a whole (reproduced below). I reached out to the Sustainable Development Solutions Network, and got explicit permission to use their data for this project if I list the following citation :

Helliwell, John F., Richard Layard, Jeffrey Sachs, and Jan-Emmanuel De Neve, eds. 2020. World Happiness Report 2020. New York: Sustainable Development Solutions Network


OECD Income Inequality Dataset

This is a supplemental dataset I will use from the Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development about income inequality in different countries: OECD Income Inequality Data. This dataset gives a measure of the amount of income inequality by country by year, which I will use to compare with different countries' happiness scores.

Accessed

April 13, 2020

Rows

2,581

OECD Income Inequality

Breaks down income inequality by country in recent years, measured by the GINI coefficient. A value of 0 means total equality of income, and a value of 1 means total inequality.


Relevant Columns

Column Name Description
LOCATION Name of the country (as a 3-letter code).
Example: ISL
MEASURE Whether a row gives the GINI coefficient or some other measure.
Example: GINI
TIME Year when the row refers to.
Example: 2015
Value The GINI score itself.
Example: 0.257

License

OECD's Terms and Conditions says that "You can extract from, download, copy, adapt, print, distribute, share and embed Data for any purpose, even for commercial use. You must give appropriate credit to the OECD by using the citation associated with the relevant Data"

OECD (2020), Income inequality (indicator). doi: 10.1787/459aa7f1-en (Accessed on 13 April 2020)


World Bank Population Data

This is a supplemental dataset I will use from the World Bank that lists the population of different countries: World Bank Population Data. This dataset gives the populations of different countries, which I will compare with different countries' happiness scores.

Accessed

April 13, 2020

Rows

264

World Bank Population, Total

Total population is based on the de facto definition of population, which counts all residents regardless of legal status or citizenship. The values shown are midyear estimates.


Relevant Columns

Column Name Description
Country Name Full name of the country.
Example: ISL
Country Code Name of the country (as a 3-letter code).
Example: ISL
[Year Number] Population at that year.
Example: 330815
